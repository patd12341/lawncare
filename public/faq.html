How often should I have my lawn mowed?

During the growing season (spring through fall), we recommend weekly mowing for optimal health and appearance. In slower growth periods or during drought conditions, bi-weekly service may be sufficient.

Weekly mowing helps maintain the proper grass height, prevents stress on your lawn, and keeps it looking professionally maintained throughout the season.

When is the best time to fertilize my lawn in Illinois?

We follow a 4-step fertilization program optimized for Illinois growing conditions:

  • Early Spring (March-April): Pre-emergent + early feeding
  • Late Spring (May-June): Growth enhancement
  • Summer (July-August): Stress protection
  • Fall (September-October): Root strengthening winterizer

Each application is timed for maximum effectiveness based on grass growth patterns and weather conditions.

When should I aerate my lawn?

The best time for lawn aeration in Illinois is during the fall (September-October) when cool-season grasses are actively growing. Early spring (March-April) is also acceptable but fall is preferred.

Signs your lawn needs aeration include:

  • Compacted soil that's hard to penetrate
  • Water pooling on the surface
  • Thin or struggling grass growth
  • Heavy thatch buildup
  • High-traffic areas showing wear

What happens if it rains on my scheduled service day?

We monitor weather conditions closely and will reschedule service if conditions aren't suitable for quality work. We never mow wet grass as it can damage your lawn and create an uneven cut.

If weather delays occur, we'll contact you to reschedule for the next available day when conditions are appropriate. Your lawn's health is our priority.

How do you handle grass clippings?

We offer flexible options for grass clipping management based on your preference:

  • Mulching: Clippings are finely chopped and left on the lawn to decompose naturally, providing nutrients
  • Bagging: Clippings are collected and removed from your property
  • Side Discharge: Clippings are dispersed evenly across the lawn

We'll discuss the best option for your lawn during our consultation.
